In the Spotlight

A college essay about Mrs. Slattery — The Bianchi Family

At Johnson School, I have gained so many valuable experiences and encountered many inspirational teachers that it feels impossible to choose just one. I even wrote my college essay about how Johnson was life-changing because of its strong community, righteous values, and especially the Peacemakers program. At the center of it all is the heart of Johnson: Mrs. Slattery. Every morning and afternoon, you could find Mrs. Slattery at the doors, greeting students with a big smile or sending us off with encouragement. She somehow knew where every student needed to be at any moment, whether it was reminding someone about an afternoon playdate, managing traffic control on the blacktop, or going out of her way to give my younger sisters a sticker. She kept everything running so smoothly, yet she always made time for people. She was the face of the Jaguar community, and helped the motto "Once a Jaguar, always a Jaguar" be meaningful to me. Johnson built the foundation for my life, and Mrs. Slattery represents that foundation — one I will carry with me to college and beyond.

— John Bianchi & The Bianchi Family 💙

Jordan Hoffman — DJ Slatts on the Air

One of my favorite memories of Mrs. Slattery — also known as DJ Slatts to many at Johnson — takes me back to a cold winter day when the snow began to fall. In the middle of the day, we decided to have a little fun by creating the Johnson School Snowflake Trivia game which we broadcasted over the loudspeaker like a radio show. Mrs. Slattery got on the loudspeaker and announced with full flair: "This is DJ Slatts coming to you live from Johnson Elementary School! Get ready for our snowflake trivia challenge — cool prizes are up for grabs! Teachers and Jaguars, grab your phones and dial 2200 with the correct answer. Remember, the 5th caller with the right answer wins — so get ready to ring in and claim your prize!" As the calls started pouring in, DJ Slatts was right there at the phone, spinning into action like a pro on the airwaves. "Hello, Johnson Elementary, this is DJ Slatts — are you the lucky caller?" she'd ask with perfect timing, each response delivered with that signature mix of flair and humor. We laughed endlessly, but what stayed with me most was the joy it brought to the whole school — one of those simple, spontaneous moments that made the day so joyful. Happy 70th birthday, Mrs. Slattery! Your generosity, care and love have made a difference in the lives of everyone around you.

— Jordan Hoffman

Ann McAvinn — You Were Everything

Robin, you were truly the heart and soul of Johnson. You wore many hats — and wore them all with grace, humor, and heart. You greeted everyone with a smile that instantly made the school feel warm and welcoming. You were our "DJ Slatts," always playing music — whether it was a holiday or just an ordinary day — and somehow turning the office into a place of joy, complete with spontaneous dance parties. You were also a counselor, comforting children who came in for a Band-Aid or worried about a forgotten lunch, and supporting staff as they navigated life's challenges. You were our biggest cheerleader, celebrating the academic successes of students and even the accomplishments of staff members' children. I'll never forget you going out of your way to attend my son's playoff game in another town — that's just who you are. Because of you, Johnson wasn't just a school — it was a community filled with warmth, care, and connection. It was as special as it was because of you. Happy Birthday, my friend.

— Ann McAvinn

Catherine Brunell — Director of Every Operation Everywhere

I love Mrs. Slattery as DJSlats but I love her even more as the Director of Every Operation Everywhere. She knew where the paper plates were — and probably had a rough estimate of how many there were in the basement PTO dungeon, maybe even by color. She knew who still had a lunch order to turn in and that your CORI was going to be invalid in 3 days. Robin also knew when you needed a hug and when you needed to hear a really funny story. I was recently organizing a group of high schoolers to come to the State House with us on a field trip and learned I needed a staff member. Without even thinking about it, I said, "Can I have Mrs. Slattery?" She is the backbone, the heart, the soul, the keeper of all memories and the unleashing of fun. And she will make sure a bouncy house is delivered on time and that they leave the right length of cord to be plugged into exactly the outlet she has already determined would be best. God, I love her. Happy Birthday Robin!

— Catherine Brunell

An Anonymous Neighbor — 34 Years Next Door

Robin is the best neighbor anyone could ever have. I have lived next door to her and her children for 34 years. Of all the regular lovely things — the chats, the this and the that which regularly occur between people who live close together — there was one event I can never, ever forget. On the day I lost my sweet son, I also lost my self control in that unimaginably crushing moment of immense disbelief and grief, and Robin came running to hold me together as much as possible. She was still wet from a warm shower she was enjoying on that fateful Memorial Day but did not hesitate to come running to my house and hold me from collapse. I will remember this moment on that terrible day for the rest of my life. Thank you, Rob. I love you dearly.

— A Neighbor, with love
